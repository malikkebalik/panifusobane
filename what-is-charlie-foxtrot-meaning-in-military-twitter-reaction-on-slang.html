What Is Charlie Foxtrot? Meaning In Military

Charlie Foxtrot is military slang for a tumultuous scenario, i.e., a cluster f***.

In the 1950s, the North Atlantic Treaty Organization developed a phonetic alphabet adopted by military and civilian radio communication. To reduce miscommunication, the alphabet employs words instead of letters.

In the NATO phonetic alphabet, Charlie represents the letter C, whereas Foxtrot stands for the letter F. The result is a Charlie Foxtrot for the compound cluster f***. 

Charlie Foxtrot began as a metaphor for a bad operation during the Vietnam War in the 1960s.

Thanks to warriors coming back home from service and popular entertainment portrayals of the military, Charlie Foxtrot and another sweary military lingo like SNAFU made their way into the civilian sector.
