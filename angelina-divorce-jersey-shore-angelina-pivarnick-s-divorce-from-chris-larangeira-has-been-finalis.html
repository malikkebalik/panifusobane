About Angelina Pivarnick

Pivarnick worked as a waitress prior to joining the Jersey Shore cast. On December 4, 2009, Jersey Shore made its debut. After refusing to work her shift at the T-shirt shop, she was kicked out of the beach home in the third episode of season one. After numerous altercations with other cast members Michael Sorrentino and Nicole “Snooki” Polizzi during season two of Jersey Shore in Miami Beach, Florida, Pivarnick once more left the house. She did not come back for season 3. Throughout the fifth and sixth seasons, Pivarnick made cameos.

The song “I’m Hot” by Pivarnick was released in 2010.

The reality series Couples Therapy on VH1 featured her and her boyfriend in its first season, which debuted on March 25, 2012.

Pivarnick participated in a six-person tag team bout on the professional wrestling show Total Nonstop Action Wrestling in March 2011.

Pivarnick made a fleeting appearance in the “Dirty Style” music video by the rap duo Dacav5 in 2011.

She additionally participated in the reality dating program Excused. Sergio decided to attempt dating her after she advanced to the final round. Later, in August 2011, she released a new song titled “Gotta Go Out” with Tony Hanson “Fenix” as Miss AP. The song peaked at No. 37 on the Billboard Dance/Club Play Songs list in the United States.

She debated music artist Adam Barta in September 2012 about homosexual marriage, which she claimed to be against. She eventually changed her mind after Jersey Shore co-star JWoww lambasted her on Twitter and apologized in front of the public via TMZ. In an effort to heal fences with the LGBT community, Pivarnick and Barta released a track in December titled “Serendipity.”

Pivarnick and her fiancé Chris Larangeira made an appearance on the program How Far Is Tattoo Far? in 2018.

Pivarnick appeared as a guest on Double Shot at Love in 2019

Pivarnick first appeared on Jersey Shore: Family Vacation in 2018 as a recurring guest before joining the group permanently in 2019.

Angelina Pivarnick Personal Life

After dating Chris Larangeira for a year, Pivarnick and Larangeira got engaged on January 12, 2018. They tied the knot on November 20, 2019. After two years of marriage, Larangeira filed for divorce in February 2022, alleging irreconcilable differences.

Pivarnick works for the FDNY in Staten Island and is a certified emergency medical technician.
She resolved a case in 2020 against a lieutenant she said had harassed her sexually.
